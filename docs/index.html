How AI is quietly changing knowledge work

5 ways AI is actually changing how knowledge work gets done (not the hype version): 1. First drafts are free now. The cost of going from blank page to something editable just hit zero. 2. Junior researchers are closing the gap. A good prompt plus a capable model beats two years of domain experience on many research tasks. 3. Meetings are getting shorter. Not because AI is in the room — because people show up with pre-synthesized context instead of raw chaos. 4. The bottleneck moved. It's no longer "can we find the information." It's "can we ask the right question." 5. Code review is the new writing feedback. Engineers are using AI to pressure-test logic before it ever hits a teammate. None of this is magic. It's compression. AI is compressing the time between thinking and doing. The people winning aren't the ones using the most tools. They're the ones who figured out which part of their workflow was pure overhead — and eliminated it. The skill that matters now isn't prompting. It's knowing what's worth doing at all.
