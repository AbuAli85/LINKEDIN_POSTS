The hidden ROI of AI: time-to-decision, not headcount
✏ State a contrarian take: argue the opp…
Most companies are measuring AI ROI wrong. Not because they're bad at math. Because they're asking the wrong question. The metric everyone tracks: headcount reduction. The metric that actually moves the business: how fast a decision gets made. Here's the gap I keep seeing. A team ships an AI tool that saves 10 hours a week per analyst. Leadership calls it a win. But those 10 hours get absorbed into more prep work, more reports, more backlog. Nothing actually changed at the decision layer. The companies quietly winning with AI aren't the ones with the lowest headcount. They're the ones where a product call that used to take 3 weeks of data gathering now takes 3 hours. That compression is where the real money is. Faster decisions mean faster experiments. Faster experiments mean faster learning. That compounds in ways headcount reduction never does. If you want to audit your AI investment honestly, ask this: what decision can you make today that you couldn't make last quarter — not because you have more people, but because you have the answer faster? If you can't name one, the ROI isn't there yet. What's the one decision in your org that's still painfully slow despite all the tools you've bought? #AIStrategy #ProductLeadership #EngineeringLeadership #ArtificialIntelligence #TechLeadership
